Law Of Conservation of Mass

"In any chemical reaction ,the total mass of the reacting substancesis equal to the total mass of products of the reaction -provided masses,are measured under similar condtions"

Explanation

If A & B react to form C & D then according to -Law of conservation of mass
Mass of A+Mass of B=Mass of C+Mass of D
Example:
In formation of Iron[II] sulphide -by heating iron and sulphur . 56 parts of Fe&32 parts of sulphur chemically combine to form -88 parts of FeS
Mass of iron+ Mass of sulphur= Mass of iron [II] sulphide
56 +32 =88
